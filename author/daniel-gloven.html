Week 1

Day 1 - Python Intro

Today started with introductions from the instructors and students. Our cohort is 21 strong and the largest Denver Data Science cohort to date. It was great to learn about everyone's backgrounds. Many have interesting backgrounds in engineering, science and programming. Everyone has one thing in common, though ... they're all super smart. I'm psyched to be part of such a great group.

Right away, I realized that this class is an accelerated program. After introductions, we went straight in to a 2 hour assessment on Python, probability, numpy, pandas, SQL, and some general statistics. They wanted to get an idea of how much we had prepared for the class. I was able to complete all of the Python and SQL questions, but did not get to the math. Going in to the program, I know statistics is my weak point compared to programming and databases. I feel right at home on the command line, and less so on the math. I've taken plenty of difficult math in my life, but no pure statistics classes.

The day finished up with a Python pair programming assignment. It was about writing efficient code, among other things. Some useful functions were izip() and iteritems().

All in all, day 1 started at 8:00am and finished at 5:30pm. Little did I know that this would the be shortest day...
